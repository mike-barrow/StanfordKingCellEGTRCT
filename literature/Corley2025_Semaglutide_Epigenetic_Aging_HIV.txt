Corley MJ, Dwaraka V, Pang APS, Labbato D, Smith R, Eckard AR, McComsey GA.
Semaglutide Slows Epigenetic Aging in People with HIV-associated lipohypertrophy:
Evidence from a Randomized Controlled Trial.
medRxiv. 2025 Jul 14. doi: 10.1101/2025.07.09.25331038
PMID: 40791720 | PMC: PMC12338914

VERIFIED DATA FROM PAPER
========================

1. TRIAL DURATION: 32 weeks (CONFIRMED)
   - "post-hoc epigenetic analysis of a 32-week, double-blind, placebo-controlled
     phase 2b trial"
   - "Using paired peripheral-blood methylomes collected at baseline and 32 weeks"
   - 8-week dose titration + 24 weeks at 1.0 mg = 32 weeks total

2. NUMBER OF PARTICIPANTS:
   - 108 randomized (54 semaglutide, 54 placebo)
   - 8 participants (15%) withdrew from each group
   - 92 completed longitudinal assessments
   - EPIGENETIC ANALYSIS: 84 total (45 semaglutide, 39 placebo)
     (biobanked PBMC samples available at both timepoints)

3. EFFECT SIZES FOR EPIGENETIC CLOCKS (adjusted for sex, BMI, hsCRP, sCD163):
   Clock                    Effect Size    P-value
   ----------------------  -------------  --------
   PCGrimAge               -3.1 years     P = 0.007
   GrimAge V1              -1.4 years     P = 0.02
   GrimAge V2              -2.3 years     P = 0.009
   PhenoAge                -4.9 years     P = 0.004
   DunedinPACE             -0.09 units    P = 0.01
                            (~9% slower pace)
   OMICmAge                -2.2 years     P = 0.009
   RetroAge                -2.2 years     P = 0.030
   Intrinsic Capacity      unchanged      P = 0.31
   
   Eleven organ-system clocks showed concordant decreased aging with
   semaglutide, most prominently inflammation, brain, and heart.

4. SEMAGLUTIDE MECHANISM OF ACTION (as stated in paper):
   - "Semaglutide is a once-weekly GLP-1 receptor agonist"
   - "Semaglutide, a once-weekly glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor
     agonist, has emerged as a transformative therapeutic for its marked weight
     reduction and cardiometabolic benefits, including reductions in visceral
     adipose tissue"
   - "The trial aimed to evaluate the effects of the GLP-1 receptor agonist
     semaglutide on adipose tissue quantity and distribution"
   - Described as "a candidate gerotherapeutic" - proposed to slow biological
     aging through its metabolic and anti-inflammatory effects
   - No specific text about glycemic control in this epigenetic analysis, but
     the parent trial (Eckard et al. 2024, Lancet Diabetes Endocrinol) excluded
     participants with diabetes; baseline glycemic control was normal
     (median HbA1c 5.5%)

5. STUDY POPULATION:
   - Adults with HIV-1, stable ART >=12 weeks, HIV-1 RNA <400 copies/mL
   - BMI >=25 kg/m2, with lipohypertrophy
   - No type 1/2 diabetes or cardiovascular disease
   - Mean age: 49 +/- 12 years
   - 42% women, 58% Black, 38% White
   - Median BMI: 32.9 kg/m2
   - Median HbA1c: 5.5%
   - Median HOMA-IR: 2.9

6. KEY FINDING (abstract):
   "These findings provide, to our knowledge, the first clinical-trial evidence
   that semaglutide modulates validated epigenetic biomarkers of aging, justifying
   further evaluation of GLP-1 receptor agonists for health-span extension."

7. UPDATE NOTE:
   A peer-reviewed version has been published in Nature Communications
   (2026 May 19, PMID: 42156721, doi: 10.1038/s41467-026-72861-3).
