## 1. EPIDEMIOLOGY — EGT Levels Predict Human Outcomes

### Smith et al. 2020 — Malmö Diet and Cancer Study ⭐ PRIMARY
📁 REPO: egt_mortality_malmo_20yr.pdf
Heart. PMID 31672783. DOI: 10.1136/heartjnl-2019-315835

N=3,236 participants without CVD or diabetes. Median follow-up **21.4 years**.
843 deaths, 603 CVD events, 362 diabetes cases.

**Key finding:** EGT was the strongest dietary biomarker linked to a health-conscious
diet. Per 1 SD higher plasma EGT:
- All-cause mortality: HR 0.86 (p = 4 × 10⁻⁵)
- Cardiovascular mortality: HR 0.79 (p = 0.002)
- Coronary heart disease: HR 0.85 (p = 0.01)
- Independent of age, sex, smoking, BMI, blood pressure, cholesterol

**What this means:** This is the single most compelling piece of evidence that EGT
is relevant to human aging. If you had to show one paper to justify the study, this is it.

### Paul 2022 — "EGT: A Stress Vitamin" (General Review) ⭐
📁 SITE: EGT_General.pdf
Antioxidants & Redox Signaling, 2022. DOI: 10.1089/ars.2021.0043

Comprehensive review arguing EGT should be classified as a "stress vitamin" —
essential for optimal health but not strictly required for survival. Covers:
- EGT crosses blood-brain barrier (relevant to cognitive endpoints)
- Neuroprotective in Alzheimer's and Parkinson's models
- Anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties
- Depletion accelerates aging phenotypes in vivo
- Levels decline with age, suggesting supplementation may be beneficial

**What this means:** Good broad-context review for the "what is EGT" part of the
presentation. Frames EGT as conditionally essential.

## 2. MOLECULAR MECHANISMS — How EGT Works at the Cellular Level

### D'Onofrio et al. 2016 — EGT + SIRT1/SIRT6 + High Glucose ⭐
📁 REPO: donofrio_2016_egt_sirt6_highglucose.pdf
Free Radic Biol Med. PMID 27101740. DOI: 10.1016/j.freeradbiomed.2016.04.013

**Key finding:** EGT (0.5 mM) upregulates SIRT1 and SIRT6 under high-glucose
conditions, blocking endothelial senescence. **SIRT6 silencing by siRNA abolishes
the protective effect entirely** — causal proof.

Mechanism: EGT reduces ROS, downregulates p66Shc and NF-κB, preserves SIRT6
expression. SIRT6 is a master regulator at the intersection of glucose metabolism
(HIF1α, G6PC) and chromatin silencing (H3K9, H3K56 deacetylation).

**What this means:** This is the mechanistic bridge between EGT, metabolic health,
and epigenetic regulation. Directly relevant to Snyder's interest in glycemic
control + aging.

---

### Sprenger et al. 2025 — EGT Activates MPST (First Direct Target) ⭐
📁 REPO: egt_mpst_mitochondria_cellmetab_2025.pdf
📁 SITE: EGT_Exercise_Study.pdf (same paper)
Cell Metabolism, 2025.

**Key finding:** Identified MPST (3-mercaptopyruvate sulfurtransferase) as the
**first direct molecular target of EGT**. EGT accumulates in muscle mitochondria
during exercise, directly binds and activates MPST, boosting mitochondrial
respiration and exercise performance.

This is a completely separate mechanism from SIRT6 — EGT hits multiple targets.

**What this means:** A clean, concrete molecular target story. EGT is not a vague
antioxidant — it has a defined binding partner. This is the kind of mechanism
Snyder will appreciate.

### Zhao & Qi 2026 — EGT as Geroprotector (Hallmarks Review) ⭐
📁 REPO: zhao_qi_2026_egt_geroprotector_review.pdf
Ageing Research Reviews, 2026. PMID 42107722. DOI: 10.1016/j.arr.2026.103158

Systematic review (2005-2025) mapping EGT onto all 12+ Lopez-Otin hallmarks
of aging:
- Telomere maintenance — EGT preserves telomere length
- Mitochondrial integrity — MPST activation, ROS reduction
- NRF2-mediated cytoprotective response — activates antioxidant genes
- Epigenetic regulation — SIRT1/SIRT6, chromatin modification
- Neuroprotection — crosses BBB, reduces neuroinflammation
- Metabolic dysfunction — SIRT6 links to glucose homeostasis
- Network pharmacology shows multi-target effects

**What this means:** A comprehensive map showing EGT touches multiple aging
hallmarks simultaneously. Use this to show breadth without having to cite
20 individual papers.

## 3. CLINICAL TRIALS — EGT in Humans

### Yau, Cheah et al. 2024 — EGT-MCI Pilot (Cognition) ⭐
📁 REPO: yau_2024_egt_mci_pilot.pdf
J Alzheimers Dis, 2024. PMID 39544014. DOI: 10.1177/13872877241291253

- 25 mg EGT 3x/week (~10.7 mg/day) vs placebo, **1 year**
- MCI patients, mean age ~70, N not large (pilot)
- Safe and well-tolerated, no toxicity
- **Improved Rey Auditory Verbal Learning Test (AVLT) learning**
- Stabilized neurofilament light (NfL) levels
- Primary dose justification for our 30 mg/day

**What this means:** This is the longest human EGT trial and shows cognitive
stabilization. Supports both our dose choice and cognitive endpoint.

### Katsube et al. 2022 — EGT Sleep Trial ⭐
📁 SITE: EGT_Sleep.pdf
J Functional Foods, 2022. DOI: 10.1016/j.jff.2022.105165

- RCT: **20 mg EGT/day for 4 weeks**, adults with sleep complaints
- EGT significantly improved sleep difficulties (survey + EEG)
- Increased N2 stage (deep sleep), decreased N1 stage and waking after sleep onset
- Mechanisms: inhibits histamine N-methyltransferase, aldehyde dehydrogenase;
  binds α3β4 nicotinic acetylcholine receptor
- Serum metabolome: reduced glutamate, enhanced lipid metabolism

**What this means:** Direct human RCT data. EGT affects sleep through multiple
well-defined mechanisms. Supports our PSQI sleep endpoint.

---

### EGT 120mg/day Ovarian Reserve Study (2026)
📁 (verified from PubMed — PMID 42310611)
BMC Women's Health, 2026.

- Open-label, N=40 women aged 35-45 with suboptimal ovarian reserve
- **120 mg/day EGT for 3 menstrual cycles** — safe, no AEs
- AMH increased from 1.81 to 2.46 ng/mL (+0.65, p=0.018)
- PSQI sleep scores improved (6.89 → 5.50, p<0.0001)
- Authors note: open-label, needs placebo-controlled confirmation

**What this means:** The highest human dose tested to date. Safe at 4× our dose.
This is useful data if Snyder pushes for higher dosing.

## 4. PRECEDENT — Metabolic Interventions Shift Clocks

### Corley et al. 2025 (now Nat Commun 2026) — Semaglutide Slows Clocks ⭐
📁 REPO: Corley2025_Semaglutide_Epigenetic_Aging_HIV.xml (full text)
📁 REPO: Corley2025_Semaglutide_Epigenetic_Aging_HIV.txt (summary)
medRxiv → Nature Communications 2026. PMID 40791720 / 42156721.

- **32 weeks**, double-blind, N=84 (45 semaglutide, 39 placebo)
- HIV+ adults with lipohypertrophy, non-diabetic (HbA1c 5.5%, mean age 49)
- GLP-1 receptor agonist — primarily metabolic intervention

**Verified effect sizes (adjusted mean differences):**
| Clock | Effect | p-value |
|-------|--------|---------|
| GrimAge2 | -2.3 years | 0.009 |
| PhenoAge | -4.9 years | 0.004 |
| DunedinPACE | -0.09 units (~9% slower) | 0.01 |
| PCGrimAge | -3.1 years | 0.007 |
| OMICmAge | -2.2 years | 0.009 |
| Intrinsic Capacity | unchanged | 0.31 |

11 organ-system clocks showed concordant decreased aging.

**What this means:** A purely metabolic intervention shifted all major clocks
within 8 months. This is the key precedent showing the metabolic → epigenetic
aging axis is real. Directly supports Snyder's interest in metabolic interventions
and the rationale for adding a comparator compound.
