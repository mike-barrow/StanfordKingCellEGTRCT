# EGT RCT Pitch — Likely Q&A (Snyder Group)

## 1. What was the safe dose established in the 2017 study?
Cheah and Halliwell 2017 established that oral EGT supplementation is safe at up to 30 mg/day over 12 weeks in humans, with no adverse events or laboratory abnormalities — confirmed since at 120 mg/day for three months (PMID 42310611, 2026).

## 2. Is ergothioneine water soluble?
Yes, highly — EGT is a zwitterionic thione with >10 mg/mL aqueous solubility, which is why it reaches millimolar concentrations in erythrocytes without precipitating or requiring a carrier.

## 3. What is the pathway for histone wrapping and DNA repair?
EGT upregulates SIRT6, which deacetylates H3K9 and H3K56 at telomeres and double-strand break sites, enabling chromatin compaction and the recruitment of DNA-PKcs and Ku70/80 for non-homologous end joining — SIRT6 silencing abolishes the protective effect entirely (D'Onofrio 2016).

## 4. Why three clocks? Why not just one?
GrimAge2 captures mortality risk (trained on time-to-death), PhenoAge captures metabolic/clinical age (trained on nine lab biomarkers), and DunedinPACE captures rate of aging (years of aging per chronological year) — divergence between them tells you mechanism, not just effect size.

## 5. Why 30 mg/day? How was the dose chosen?
Tian et al. 2021 tested 5 and 30 mg/day in a dose-finding RCT and both were safe and well-tolerated; 30 mg was the top tested dose at protocol design time, and it is approximately 10× the median dietary intake in Western populations, sufficient to raise plasma levels measurably without entering untested territory.

## 6. Why 12 months? Is that enough to see clock changes?
Corley 2025/2026 shifted GrimAge2 (−2.3 yr, p=0.009), PhenoAge (−4.9 yr, p=0.004), and DunedinPACE (−0.09, p=0.01) in 32 weeks — 12 months is conservative for a nutraceutical with a milder effect size and covers one full annual methylation-drift cycle (Ricceri 2014).

## 7. Why healthy 50-70 year olds? Why not a diseased population?
Clock validation is strongest in this age range (no ceiling effects from advanced age, enough epigenetic drift to detect slowing), and showing that EGT slows aging in a healthy population is a cleaner geroscience claim than showing it in a diseased one — the mechanism may generalize better to primary prevention.

## 8. How were EGT levels measured in the completed RCTs?
Plasma EGT by LC-MS/MS in all cases — the method is well-established with published protocols (detection limit ~10 nM), and we would use the same validated assay in-house.

## 9. What is your power?
80% to detect δ=0.50 in GrimAge2 EAA at α=0.05 two-sided with N=128 evaluable, padded to N=160 for 20% attrition — sufficient for the hierarchical gatekeeping procedure (GrimAge2→PhenoAge→DunedinPACE).

## 10. Will you genotype OCTN1? Won't that matter for who responds?
Yes — SLC22A4 (OCTN1) genotyping at screening; only complete loss-of-function mutations are excluded (<1% of population), and the L503F common variant will be used for pre-specified subgroup analysis of plasma EGT levels and clock response.

## 11. How does EGT compare to semaglutide or other interventions that shift clocks?
Semaglutide is a potent GLP-1 agonist with systemic metabolic effects; EGT is a dietary amino acid with a defined molecular target (MPST) and a chromatin-based pathway (SIRT6) — the effect size will almost certainly be smaller, but the safety profile allows chronic use without monitoring, and the mechanism is orthogonal, which matters if the question is "what slows aging" rather than "what drug works."
