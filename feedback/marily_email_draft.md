Thanks again for the call. I wanted to follow up on the specific items you raised.

On glycemic control and EGT: the direct evidence is D'Onofrio et al. (2016, Free Radic Biol Med). EGT upregulates SIRT1 and SIRT6 in endothelial cells under high-glucose conditions, blocking senescence. SIRT6 silencing abolished the effect entirely. A 2026 paper in Phytomedicine also shows EGT alleviates hepatic steatosis in NAFLD models via PCYT2 modulation, which touches liver metabolism. I have PDFs for both.

On the senescence paper: Zhao and Qi (2026, Ageing Research Reviews) is a systematic review mapping EGT onto the Lopez-Otin hallmarks of aging framework. It covers mitochondrial integrity, NRF2, telomere maintenance, neurodegeneration, and metabolic dysfunction. I have the PDF.

On the topic of performance and EGT, a 2025 Cell Metabolism paper (Sprenger et al.) shows EGT accumulates in muscle mitochondria during exercise and directly activates MPST to boost mitochondrial respiration and exercise performance. It identifies the first direct molecular target of EGT. Worth a look given your interest in exercise effects.

On methylation clock variability: the Belsky DunedinPACE paper (eLife, 2022) reports test-retest ICC of 0.96 (95% CI 0.93-0.98), which is excellent. That trial found significant clock effects with N=84 total over 32 weeks, suggesting our proposed N=160 over 12 months is adequately powered.
